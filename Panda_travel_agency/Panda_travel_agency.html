Eastern State Penitentiary

History

This halloween, travel to the spookiest, most haunted place the United States. The building that was once home to the most famous and expensive prison in the world is now open for your horror experience. Eastern State was the first true "penitentiary" and is known for the true regret it inspired in the hearts of its prinsoners through torturous tactics. The prison once contained some of America's most notorious prisoners such as "Slick Willie" Sutton and Al Capone.

The prison is haunted by the spirits of the inmates who lived and died within its walls. These inmates were kept in complete solitary, which led to serious mental illness. The penitentiary was founded as a place where criminals would find God, but in reality they only grew further from him.

Torture at the Penitentiary

  • Water Bath method: Inmates who misbehaved were dunked in icy water, then hung on the wall and left alone for the night. In the winter this punishment was worse because ice would form on the prisoner's skin as the temperature dipped below freezing.
  • Iron Gag: This punishment was used on inmates who ignored the no communication policy. An iron collar was secured around the tongue and chains were attached that were shackled to each wrist. If the inmate moved enough, their tongue would be ripped. Very often the inmate bled enough that they died and the collar was never removed.
  • Mad Chair: Inmate is strapped into a chair with leather straps and left for days without rations. By the end circulation would nearly stop due to the suffocating straps.
  • The Hole: A deep, lightless, airless pit dug under block #14 that inmates would be placed in for days and sometimes weeks with minimal amounts of bread and water to survive. They competed with rats and other pests for this food supple

Accounts of Paranormal Activity

One locksmith tells a particularly terrifying tale of his experience at the penitentiary. He was working to remove a 140 year old lock from a cell door when suddenly he was immobilized. Tortured faces appeared on the walls and shifting forms circled around him in the cell block, while one of them seemed to call to the locksmith. Many people believe that the force that made him unable to move was the escaping ghosts of the prisoners.
